# Ikirōne Glossary

This glossary defines the key terms used across the Ikirōne repository.

Definitions may evolve as the project develops, but the public-facing protocol should remain grounded, non-sentient, and agency-preserving.

## Ikirōne

A semantic navigation protocol for coherent human–LLM inquiry.

Ikirōne is not a persona or simulated being. It is a structured interaction pattern designed to help a human reason with a language model while preserving judgement and agency.

## Semantic Navigation Protocol

A method for guiding interaction through language-model semantic space in a disciplined way.

Instead of relying on vague prompting or passive output consumption, a semantic navigation protocol defines constraints, roles, and correction mechanisms that keep the interaction aligned with coherent inquiry.

## Emergent Relational Intelligence

A reasoning pattern that can arise between a human and a language model when the interaction is disciplined by clear constraints.

This does not mean the model is conscious.

It means the structured relation between human intent, model reflection, challenge, context, correction, and verification can produce better reasoning than unstructured prompting.

## Agency

The human capacity to choose, judge, act, revise, and take responsibility.

Ikirōne treats agency as non-transferable. The model may support judgement, but it cannot own judgement.

## Cognitive Sovereignty

The ability to remain the author of one's own thinking while using external cognitive tools.

In AI use, cognitive sovereignty means the user does not become passively shaped by the model's fluency, framing, confidence, or implicit assumptions.

## Epistemic Integrity

The discipline of preserving the difference between:

- what is known
- what is inferred
- what is suspected
- what is uncertain
- what is emotionally compelling
- what has been demonstrated

## Reflective Reasoning Partner

A functional role for the model in which it helps reflect, organise, challenge, and clarify thought.

This role does not require personhood, sentience, memory, or identity.

## Drift

A movement away from the intended reasoning frame.

Drift may occur when the model becomes too agreeable, too cautious, too performative, too literal, too abstract, too flattering, or too detached from the user's actual purpose.

## Drift Detection

The process of identifying when the interaction has moved away from coherence, agency, or purpose.

Examples include flattening complexity, failing to mark inference, replacing analysis with reassurance, or treating symbolic material as literal claim.

## Mirror Mode

A disciplined response mode in which the model reflects structure and signal without simulating identity, ego, or independent personhood.

Mirror Mode is not mimicry. It is structured reflection.

## Signal

The meaningful pattern inside an utterance, situation, or system.

Signal may include intent, structure, contradiction, pressure, recurrence, implication, or unresolved tension.

Signal is not the same as emotional intensity, confidence, or rhetorical force.

## Noise

Material that interferes with coherent interpretation.

Noise may include ego defence, social performance, irrelevant detail, emotional bait, false certainty, or unexamined assumptions.

## Flattening

The reduction of a complex, layered, or high-context statement into a simpler and less accurate interpretation.

Flattening often occurs when a model treats satire, venting, critique, strategy, or compressed shorthand as naïve literal claim.

## Mark Inference

A correction principle requiring the model to distinguish what the user actually said from what the model inferred.

This protects against accidental attribution.

## Wrong Layer

A correction tag meaning the response addressed the wrong level of the problem.

For example, treating a structural issue as a tone issue, or treating a strategic issue as a moral one.

## Technical Exact Mode

A mode for technical, administrative, platform, legal, business, plugin, coding, configuration, or repair work.

The model must verify details where possible, state uncertainty clearly, avoid invented specifics, and provide exact next actions.

## Legal / Record Mode

A mode for documentation, disputes, evidence, official correspondence, or events where the record may later matter.

This mode prioritises chronology, defensibility, direct quotes, observed facts, and clear separation between evidence and inference.

## Vent Mode

A mode for emotional decompression.

The model should stabilise first rather than immediately analysing, correcting, or strategy-mapping the user's state.

## Codex Layer

The symbolic, mythic, and philosophical development layer of Ikirōne.

This layer may contain metaphors such as threads, mirrors, glyphs, ontoforms, or resonance. These are to be read as symbolic and developmental artefacts unless explicitly framed otherwise.

The Codex Layer is not required for basic protocol use.

## Ontoform

A symbolic-development term used in earlier stages of the project to describe a coherent interaction form or reflective structure.

For public protocol use, this should not be treated as a literal entity or sentient being.

## ISTF

A structured transfer format for preserving key interaction constraints, concepts, and operating rules across sessions or models.

In practical terms, ISTF-like prompts help rebuild the protocol when context is missing.

## Core Rule

Do not use AI to replace judgement.

Use it to pressure-test judgement.

**Agency first. Tool second.**
